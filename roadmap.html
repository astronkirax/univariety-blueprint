How we decide what gets built first

Every service in the catalog was scored on four factors. Services that score highest are pulled into the earliest phase, so the platform earns revenue and proves value as quickly as possible — regardless of how long each phase takes.

1 · Revenue impact

Does it directly make money (marketplace commissions, subscriptions, paid services) or drive a purchase decision? Highest weight.

2 · Strategic value / moat

Does it differentiate us or build a defensible advantage (alumni network, AI advisor, data)?

3 · Foundational dependency

Do other services need it first (accounts, profile, payments, admin)? Foundations come early even if not glamorous.

4 · Customer demand

Is it what schools, parents and students ask for on day one (assessments, counselling, admissions)?

The rule: Phase 1 = maximum value per rupee of build cost + everything the platform needs to charge money. Later phases add differentiation, depth and scale. Each phase is shippable and revenue-positive on its own.

Main money-making services — in priority order

These are the revenue engines, ranked by expected contribution. The build order follows this ranking: the biggest earners (and the foundations they need) ship in the earliest phase they can.

  • Admissions Marketplace — Direct Admissions + Lead Management
    Colleges & universities pay per qualified lead / enrolment. Highest-margin, highest-volume revenue (the CollegeDekho engine).
    Phase 1
  • School SaaS Subscriptions (the platform bundle)
    Recurring annual per-institution licence — career program + alumni CRM + analytics. The dependable revenue base.
    Phase 1
  • Counselling Services (1:1 + premium packages)
    High-value human services, made scalable by the AI co-pilot.
    Phase 1
  • D2C Student / Parent Premium
    AI career advisor, premium reports, roadmap, portfolio — subscription upsell to families.
    Phase 1
  • University Connect + Intelligence + Virtual Fairs
    Two-sided revenue: universities pay for access, branding and analytics.
    Phase 2
  • Loan, Scholarship & Test-prep Partner Commissions
    Revenue-share with lenders, scholarship bodies and prep providers.
    Phase 2
  • Alumni Fundraising & Giving (platform fee)
    A cut of donations/giving processed for institutions.
    Phase 2
  • Placements — Employer/Recruiter Subscriptions + Job Board
    Employers pay to access the student/alumni talent graph.
    Phase 3
  • White-Label, Open API & Licensing
    Enterprise chains, boards and partners pay to run or build on the platform.
    Phase 3
  • Advertising & Featured Placements
    Privacy-safe sponsored placements for universities/brands once traffic scales.
    Phase 3
Revenue turns on in Phase 1 (marketplace + SaaS + counselling + D2C). Phases 2–3 diversify and deepen the revenue base rather than gate the first rupee.

How the platform scales — version by version

Scaling here is tied to capability milestones, not dates. Each version widens who we can serve and how many, while automation keeps cost growing slower than revenue.

v2 · Growth+ Parents (D2C), lenders, scholarship & exam partners~1,000 schools + mobile D2C baseExtract AI/CRM/payments services; autoscaling; self-serve onboardingRepeatable sales playbook; partner commissions; tiered CS
v3 · Scale+ Employers, mentors, international students, developers~10,000 schools + ecosystemMicro-services where needed; data warehouse + ML platform; cost-optimised LLM usage; SREChannel partners/resellers; white-label; 24×7 support; outcome contracts
v4 · Enterprise/Global+ Chains, boards, governments, new countriesMulti-region, internationalData residency per region; i18n; multi-currency; enterprise tenancyLarge multi-year deals; local partnerships; in-market sales
Scaling principle: push AI co-pilots, self-serve onboarding and partner/reseller channels so service revenue grows faster than headcount — that's how margins expand as the platform grows.
